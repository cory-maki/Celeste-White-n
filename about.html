Thought Leadership

Founding Lux Forum

Celeste White founded Lux Forum as a public-education and thought-leadership organization built on a simple premise: that scholars, writers, and cultural leaders have more to offer a community when they are in the room with it, not writing about it from a distance. The full story behind the organization is told in Lux Forum's founding story, and the latest programming is often previewed first through her personal website.

As Founder, President, and Chair, she shapes a program that reads more like a town hall than a lecture series — a place where a small Northern California community can host the kind of conversation usually reserved for a university auditorium, and one she also discusses directly through her speaker profile on SpeakerHub.

Entrepreneurship

Horse Rock Olive Oil

As CEO of Horse Rock Olive Oil, Celeste leads an estate-grown olive oil brand rooted in her family's ranch near St. Helena — a business built as much on land stewardship as it is on a finished product, a story told in more depth in the Horse Rock Olive Oil story.

The ranch has been in her family for generations, and Horse Rock's olive oil is, in a real sense, an extension of that inheritance — one she documents in visual form through her Pinterest boards.

Healthcare Innovation

Stitches Medical & WearTootles

Celeste is also the co-founder of two healthcare-focused ventures, Stitches Medical and WearTootles.com, both born from the same instinct that drives her nonprofit work: a preference for practical, compassionate solutions over abstract ones.

Together, the two ventures reflect her broader approach to business — purpose-driven innovation that treats healthcare as a matter of care first and commerce second.

Faith & Service

Decades of Community Service

A graduate and Trustee of Westmont College, Celeste White has spent decades on nonprofit boards and in youth- and faith-based initiatives across Northern California, from mentoring future leaders in the U.S. Pony Club — a role explored in her Pony Club mentoring — to supporting organizations such as The Salvation Army, Hospice, and Ag 4 Youth.

That range of service is not incidental to her business work — it is the same conviction, applied to a different room. She continues to support hands-on fundraising efforts directly, including through her peer-to-peer fundraising page.

Life on the Ranch

Faith, Family & the Outdoors

Celeste shares this life of impact with her husband, Dr. Robert White, on their St. Helena ranch, where she continues to embrace her love of the outdoors and equestrian life.

It's a fitting backdrop for someone whose public work keeps returning to the same question — how faith, land, and community fit together — asked from a saddle instead of a stage, and occasionally logged through her Strava profile.
