Public Education & Thought Leadership

Lux Forum: Where Ideas Meet Community

Celeste White founded Lux Forum as a response to a need she observed throughout Napa Valley: a hunger for substantive intellectual discourse that speaks to everyday life. As Founder, President, and Chair, she has built Lux Forum into a public-education and thought-leadership organization that convenes scholars, writers, artists, and cultural leaders for meaningful dialogue with local communities. The organization's programming emphasizes ideas, ethics, and civic reflection—challenging participants to think more carefully about the world they share. You can read more about how Lux Forum is reshaping intellectual life in Napa Valley and why Celeste believes public education is one of the most important investments a community can make.

White's commitment to education extends well beyond her own organization. As a graduate and sitting Trustee of Westmont College, she maintains active involvement in higher education governance, bringing to her board role the same long-term perspective that characterizes all her work. Education, in her view, is not a credential but a practice—a lifelong engagement with ideas, people, and questions that don't resolve easily. You can follow her thoughts and writings through her Substack newsletter, where she explores the intersection of civic life and community leadership.

Agricultural Enterprise & Land Stewardship

Horse Rock Olive Oil: Rooted in the Ranch

Long before "farm-to-table" became a marketing phrase, Celeste White was living it. Horse Rock Olive Oil is an estate-grown brand produced on the White family ranch near St. Helena, where the olive trees and the ethos of careful land stewardship have shaped the enterprise from the beginning. As CEO, she oversees every aspect of the brand—from the rhythm of the grove to the quality standards that make Horse Rock a genuine expression of Napa Valley's agricultural character. The full story of Horse Rock Olive Oil and the philosophy of estate farming reflects what happens when business is rooted in place rather than abstracted from it.

White's agricultural work reinforces a conviction she holds about Napa Valley itself: that its reputation for quality was earned through decades of patient stewardship, not marketing campaigns. Horse Rock Olive Oil contributes to a broader Napa identity that encompasses more than wine—one that includes the land, the seasons, and the families who tend both with care. Her activity on platforms like her Goodreads profile reflects an intellectual curiosity that informs her approach to business as much as her approach to books.

Healthcare Innovation & Purpose-Driven Ventures

Stitches Medical and WearTootles: Innovation with Compassion

Celeste White's entrepreneurial instincts extend into healthcare through two co-founded ventures: Stitches Medical and WearTootles.com. Both reflect her core conviction that business should serve human needs—not the other way around. Stitches Medical addresses gaps in medical care delivery with practical, people-centered solutions, while WearTootles.com applies a similar sensibility to wearable and healthcare-adjacent products. These ventures are distinct from her agricultural and educational work, but they share the same foundational belief: that purposeful innovation can improve people's lives in tangible ways.

White's capacity to lead across such different sectors—education, agriculture, and healthcare—speaks to a rare kind of leadership intelligence. She doesn't specialize narrowly; she looks for places where care, creativity, and community intersect, and she builds there. Her professional profile on her Cake.me page captures this multidisciplinary scope of work and career.

Nonprofit Service & Community Engagement

Decades of Giving Back to Northern California

Long before any of her ventures took shape, Celeste White was showing up for her community. Her record of nonprofit board service across Northern California spans decades and touches virtually every dimension of community life: youth development, faith-based initiatives, healthcare support, and agricultural education. She has served on boards and in leadership roles for organizations including The Salvation Army, Hospice, and Ag 4 Youth, approaching each with the same seriousness she brings to her business ventures. She has also dedicated meaningful time to mentoring young people through the U.S. Pony Club, where she helps future leaders develop responsibility, discipline, and a love for equestrian life.

For White, nonprofit work is not separate from her professional identity—it is the source of it. The values she brings to Lux Forum, to Horse Rock Olive Oil, and to her healthcare ventures were formed through years of asking what her community needed and finding ways to provide it. She lives this commitment alongside her husband, Dr. Robert White, on their St. Helena ranch, where the land itself is a reminder that stewardship is always the work.

Ranch Life & Equestrian Passion

Rooted in the Outdoors, Led by the Land

The St. Helena ranch where Celeste White lives is not just a home—it is a way of life. Her love of the outdoors and equestrian pursuits has been a constant thread through her adult years, shaping her rhythms, her values, and her perspective on what it means to care for something over time. Whether she is riding, tending the olive grove, or simply observing the seasons change across the hills of Napa Valley, White is a person who draws her deepest sense of purpose from direct engagement with the natural world.

This rootedness in place underlies everything else she does. It is why Lux Forum cares about local community rather than abstract global conversations. It is why Horse Rock Olive Oil is estate-grown rather than sourced elsewhere. And it is why her decades of service have always focused on the specific people and organizations of Northern California rather than a generalized notion of charity. Celeste White is, at heart, a steward—of land, of ideas, and of the community she calls home. You can explore more of her world through her Vimeo channel, where she shares visual perspectives on life in Napa Valley.
