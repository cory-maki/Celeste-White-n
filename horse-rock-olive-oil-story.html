From Ranch to Bottle: The Story Behind Horse Rock Olive Oil

📅 March 3, 2026
✎ Celeste White
⌚ 5 min read

Long before it was a brand, Horse Rock was simply home — a working ranch near St. Helena that had belonged to the family for generations.

Celeste White grew up understanding that land is not scenery; it's a responsibility. As CEO of Horse Rock Olive Oil, she has spent recent years turning that understanding into an estate-grown olive oil brand built directly on the family ranch.

Estate-Grown, Not Outsourced

The distinction matters more than it might seem. A great many olive oil labels source fruit from wherever it's cheapest that season; Horse Rock's comes from trees Celeste can walk to. That kind of proximity shapes everything from harvest timing to how closely quality can be watched, bottle by bottle.

It also means the business is, in a very literal sense, inseparable from the land it sits on — a detail she has documented in glimpses shared through her Pinterest gallery.

"You can't rush an olive harvest any more than you can rush trust with a community. Both take a season you don't control."

Running a Family Ranch Like a Business

Leading Horse Rock has required Celeste White to apply the same discipline she brings to Lux Forum and her nonprofit board work: clear priorities, patient execution, and a refusal to let scale come at the expense of quality.

That discipline shows up in small decisions as much as big ones — which grove gets pressed first, which bottling run gets held back another week — the kind of judgment that only comes from actually being on the ranch, not managing it from a spreadsheet.

Why the Ranch Comes First

For Celeste, Horse Rock Olive Oil was never meant to be separate from the rest of her life. The ranch is also where she and her husband, Dr. Robert White, live, and where her equestrian life and love of the outdoors play out day to day, threads she also writes about on her Tumblr journal.

That overlap between home and business is visible in how she talks about mentoring, too — a theme she returns to in her Pony Club mentoring story, drawn from years spent teaching young riders in the U.S. Pony Club.

Conclusion

Horse Rock Olive Oil is, in the end, a family ranch that grew into a business rather than the other way around. For the fuller picture of how it fits alongside Celeste White's other work, see Celeste White's broader work.
