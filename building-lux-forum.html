Building Lux Forum: Why Public Conversation Still Matters

📅 May 12, 2026
✎ Celeste White
⌚ 6 min read

Most public conversations happen at a distance — a lecture hall, a livestream, a book tour stop. Lux Forum was built to close that distance.

When Celeste White founded Lux Forum, she wasn't trying to build another speaker series. She was trying to fix a gap she kept noticing: the scholars, writers, and cultural leaders doing the most interesting thinking in the country rarely spent real time with the communities that thinking was supposed to serve.

A Forum Rooted in Community

Lux Forum is a public-education and thought-leadership organization, and Celeste serves as its Founder, President, and Chair. The format is deliberately unglamorous: real rooms, real questions, and guests who are expected to stay for the conversation afterward, not just the talk itself.

That approach didn't come out of nowhere. It draws on the same patient, in-person instinct that shows up throughout her public writing, including her Substack newsletter, where she expands on many of these ideas between events.

"The best ideas don't hold up in a vacuum. They hold up in a room full of people who disagree with you politely."

Scholars, Writers, and Neighbors at the Same Table

The programming reflects that philosophy. Lux Forum events are built around genuine back-and-forth rather than one-directional lectures, seating academics and authors alongside the residents, students, and civic leaders their work is actually about.

It's a model Celeste has refined over years of nonprofit board service throughout Northern California, work that taught her how much a community's appetite for big ideas depends on whether those ideas ever come with an invitation to talk back.

What Comes Next for Lux Forum

Under Celeste White's chairmanship, Lux Forum continues to expand its guest roster while holding onto the same small-room intimacy that made the format work in the first place — a balance she's discussed in recordings posted through her YouTube channel.

That same instinct for direct, unhurried engagement carries over into her other work, including the patience required to run an estate-grown business — a subject she's written about in more detail in the Horse Rock Olive Oil story.

Conclusion

Lux Forum, at its core, is a bet that public conversation is worth the extra effort of doing it in person. For more on the ideas, service, and family history behind that bet, visit Celeste White's full body of work.
